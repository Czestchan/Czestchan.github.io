Research Overview

My interest lies in the rational design of catalysis and material science for energy and industrial applications. I have a strong experimentla background in electrocatalysis (advised by Prof. Yongye Liang) and photocatalysis (advised by Prof. Meng Gu and Prof. Limin Huang). Later I turned to theories and studied molecular enginnering of molecular electrocatalyst with DFT, advised by Prof. Li Dang. In 2018 summer I worked in Prof. Alexandrova Anastassia N.'s lab at UCLA as a CSST fellow, applying particle swarm optimization (PSO) combined with DFT to model the dynamic catalytic surface at the ensemble level. Now I am working on my bachelor thesis about rational design of molecular/inorganic hybrid material for CO2RR advised by Prof. Jun Li, in collaboration with Prof. Hongjie Dai.

My current interest lies in the development and application of theoretical chemistry methods to advance the field of catalysis and material science, expecially data-driven algorithms and high-throughput computations.
